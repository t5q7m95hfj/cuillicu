14 feb

My feet are cold as I sit in bed - alone…

Happy Valentine’s Day to my single self for the umpteenth time. Why hasn’t AI bioengineered me a perfect man? Or at least matched me with an imperfect one. Are my standards too high?

No, but my expectations are.

The truth is… I don’t want a man. Not in the “I’m straight” sense, but as in: would I actually feel fulfilled by being doted on by a partner? And frankly, I don’t believe I deserve that anyway…

I <3 myself - in theory but not in practice.

My life is a conscious contradiction. It’s like I’m the moon to my own planet, barely hanging in orbit by the delicate balance of gravitational forces. I have an “objective” and out of body view of myself, but I’m still just a self. And what is a self anyway? That which is defined in relation to the other?

I am the other and the self, the one and the many, defined in relation to myself and to others.

I’m boring myself already…

Well anyway, at the store today I was reminded just how much of a capitalistically-contrived, meaning-averse, arbitrary pity of a holiday Valentine’s Day really is.

Pathetic men buying pathetic cards and pathetic flowers which will never see the sun and are supposed to symbolize eternal love but will probably be in the garbage by Friday.

One guy was getting mad at the cashier because the envelope didn’t fit his $6 Hallmark card. A piece of card-stock with a generic platitude at such an exorbitant cost. Smells like love to me!

And why don’t we philosophize anymore?

I mean, wouldn’t a truer gesture of love be to recreate a modern version of Plato’s Symposium - where we get drunk and profess our odes to the deity of Love?

Like Diomita said: Love is our humanly attempt at attaining immortality, with love being passed from parent to child, from one generation to the next, through a process of unbridled succession.

And I love platonic love - a love of love itself, as meta as Plato’s tale.

I <3 myself after all, and that’s as meta as it can get.

Well, enough rambling for now.

Goodnight Jupiter and all my moons. xoxo.
